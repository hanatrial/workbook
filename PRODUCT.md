# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The 12-person **Keluarga Sultan** team (Sulawesi Terdepan field/sales unit). They use the app mostly on phones, often outdoors or between store visits, sometimes with unreliable connectivity. Their jobs: share photos from the field (store displays, stock checks), assign and track tasks with a clear PIC (person in charge — "penanggung jawab"), see team progress at a glance, and post informal personal updates ("Cerita") to stay connected as a team, not just as coworkers.

## Product Purpose

Keluarga Sultan is a lightweight, local-first team hub that merges light task/photo coordination (photo feed, task board with PIC assignment, kanban, progress report) with a family-style social feed (Cerita: text + photos + emoji reactions + comments). It exists so a small, tight-knit field team can coordinate real work and keep their team culture alive in one place, without the overhead of enterprise project-management tools. Success = the team actually opens it daily to post updates and check tasks, not just install it once.

## Brand Personality

**Hangat & akrab, ceria & fun, premium/mewah** — warm and close-knit like family, playful and fun (casual Bahasa Indonesia, emoji reactions, personality-driven figure avatars), yet crafted with premium polish rather than looking cheap or templated. The tone is never cold or corporate. Copy stays in casual Bahasa Indonesia throughout.

## Anti-references

- Generic cold corporate SaaS dashboards (sterile, enterprise, impersonal).
- Overstimulating UI — too many competing colors, animations, or effects at once ("terlalu ramai").
- Pink/magenta in the avatar or accent palette (explicitly rejected by the team).
- Gender-stereotyped or incorrect representation in avatars — must reflect each person's actual gender (currently: Lisa is female, the rest of the team is male).

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth over corporate polish** — every decision should feel like a family made this for family, not like enterprise software.
2. **Effortless one-handed, in the field** — most usage happens on a phone outdoors between tasks; large tap targets and legibility come before visual flourish.
3. **Premium without going cold** — "mewah" is expressed through material/craft details (layered cards, soft depth, considered type) rather than minimal/sterile design.
4. **Playful restraint** — fun and personality (reactions, figure avatars, casual tone) without tipping into visual noise.
5. **Zero-friction, local-first identity** — no login wall; identity is a lightweight "pilih namamu" pick, not an account system.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- All UI copy in casual Bahasa Indonesia.
- Large, thumb-friendly tap targets sized for one-handed mobile use in the field.
- Avatars are gender-correct and free of stereotyping.
- Text must maintain readable contrast against the warm cream/light backgrounds.
- Motion (scroll reveals, transitions) should degrade gracefully — never gate content visibility behind an animation that could fail to fire.
